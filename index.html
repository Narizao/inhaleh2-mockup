The Science

What every other inhaler missed: measuring what reaches the lungs.

FiH₂ — Fraction of Inspired Hydrogen — is the percentage of H₂ in the air you actually breathe in. InhaleH₂ is the only system designed to deliver a defined, repeatable FiH₂ within the clinically studied, non-flammable range.

01 / Closed-loop

Reservoir + one-way valve

An inflatable reservoir buffers hydrogen between breaths. A one-way valve prevents room-air dilution. Concentration stays where it should.

02 / SPE/PEM cell

Platinum-coated titanium

Solid Polymer Electrolyte / Proton Exchange Membrane cell with corrosion-resistant electrodes. Pure H₂ output, session after session.

03 / Defined dose

Engineered for FiH₂

The whole system is tuned to deliver a clinically meaningful concentration that compensates for breathing variability.

What does the inhaler actually do?

InhaleH₂ uses electrolysis to produce molecular hydrogen (H₂) gas, then delivers it through a closed-loop reservoir and one-way valve mask, so the concentration you breathe stays defined and consistent across an entire session.

Is it FDA-cleared?

InhaleH₂ is not an FDA-cleared medical device. It is intended for wellness and research-aligned use, not for diagnosing, treating, curing, or preventing any disease.

How is this different from drinking hydrogen water?

Hydrogen water delivers H₂ to the gut. The InhaleH₂ system delivers it through your lungs, where rapid systemic absorption occurs — and at concentrations far above what's possible to dissolve in water.

How long is each session?

15 minutes per day is the recommended baseline protocol. The system is engineered for daily use; many customers also use it pre-workout or post-stress.

What's included with the inhaler?

The unit, mask with reservoir, electrolyte cell, distilled water reservoir, power adapter, and a printed quick-start with link to the digital protocol library.
