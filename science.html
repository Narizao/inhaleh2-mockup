Section 01

What molecular hydrogen actually is.

H₂ is the smallest, most diffusible molecule in nature. It crosses cell membranes and the blood-brain barrier with no carrier needed. In the body, it has been studied for selective antioxidant behavior — quenching the most damaging reactive oxygen species without disrupting the ones cells need for signalling.

The implication for a delivery device is simple: getting H₂ into the body matters less than how reliably you can deliver a defined amount. That's the whole problem InhaleH₂ was built to solve.

Section 02

FiH₂: the metric most companies hope you don't ask about.

FiH₂ stands for Fraction of Inspired Hydrogen — the percentage of H₂ in the air you actually inhale. It's the number that determines what reaches your lungs. Every other spec on a hydrogen product (PPM in water, total H₂ output, mL/min) is upstream of this. And every other spec is meaningless if FiH₂ at the mask is unstable.

Why it matters

Dose, not vibes

Without a defined FiH₂, a "hydrogen session" is variable from one breath to the next. You can't tune what you can't measure.

Why others miss it

Open systems leak

Without a closed loop, hydrogen escapes between breaths. Each inhale draws in room air. Concentration drops fast.

Why we built this

Engineered around it

Reservoir, valve, and cell were designed together to hold a defined FiH₂ within the clinically studied, non-flammable range.

Section 03

How the closed-loop system works.

A look at what's happening inside the device — and at the mask — during a 15-minute session.

1

Electrolysis

The SPE/PEM cell splits distilled water into H₂ and O₂ using platinum-coated titanium electrodes. Pure H₂ output, no contaminants.

2

Reservoir

An inflatable reservoir buffers H₂ between breaths so the next inhale always has the same concentration as the last.

3

One-way valve

A patented one-way valve at the mask prevents room-air dilution and ensures exhaled gas exits, not re-circulates.

4

Defined FiH₂

You inhale a defined Fraction of Inspired Hydrogen — within the clinically studied range — every breath, every session.

Glossary

Plain-language definitions.

If you ever see one of these terms in our marketing or in the literature, here's what it means.

FiH₂ — Fraction of Inspired Hydrogen

The percentage of H₂ in the gas you inhale. The most important number when measuring hydrogen delivery to the lungs.

SPE / PEM — Solid Polymer Electrolyte / Proton Exchange Membrane

The type of electrolysis cell that separates H₂ from O₂ cleanly, without producing chlorine or ozone byproducts.

Closed-loop system

A delivery system where the gas the user exhales doesn't dilute the gas they inhale, and where ambient air doesn't enter the H₂ stream.

Selective antioxidant

An antioxidant that primarily neutralizes the most damaging reactive oxygen species (like hydroxyl radicals) without interfering with signaling species (like nitric oxide).

SR&ED

Canada's Scientific Research & Experimental Development program. Federal recognition of qualifying R&D activities. InhaleH₂ has received recognition under this program for its hydrogen-delivery R&D.
